Every problem tells a story — and Stream keeps each one coherent.

In traditional EHRs, reviewing a patient means flipping through a stack of disconnected notes, trying to reconstruct the clinical narrative. In Stream, that story is already organized for you.

Each medical problem — hypertension, asthma, fatigue, depression — maintains its own **longitudinal summary**, built from the documentation you’ve already written. When you open a problem, you see its history at a glance: what changed, what’s ongoing, and what’s planned next.

These summaries are more than static text; they’re living overviews that update automatically as new encounters are created. You can scroll back through time by problem or view the same data encounter by encounter.

And because each problem summary reflects structured, problem-based documentation, Stream can instantly assemble a **Patient Summary** — a clear, unified snapshot of the entire patient, built from the most recent version of every active problem.

The result:

- No more flipping between encounters to see progress.
- No more confusion about what’s current versus outdated.
- No more copying forward just to remember what changed.

With Stream, continuity doesn’t have to be recreated — it’s already there.

Whether reviewing a single problem or the whole patient, Stream gives you an immediate, accurate, and effortless overview of care.
