Every note you write in Stream already contains the information needed to complete the administrative work that follows. Instead of treating those downstream steps as afterthoughts, Stream automates them directly from your structured documentation — so you spend less time retyping and more time moving forward.

From each assessment and plan, Stream intelligently proposes **ICD and CPT codes** that match the problems you addressed. You can review, confirm, or adjust them instantly — without switching screens or scrolling through code libraries. Everything remains transparent and clinician-controlled: Stream recommends; you decide.

That same contextual intelligence powers **referrals, letters, and forms**.

Because every note in Stream is organized by problem, the system can generate **problem-specific documents** automatically — referral letters that summarize the relevant history and plan for asthma, or FMLA forms that capture the right details for back pain — all without copying and pasting.

Stream’s automation doesn’t mean loss of nuance; it means removing repetition. Each document still reflects your words and your judgment, only faster.

And soon, Stream will extend these capabilities with **HCC recommendations** and **Task Management**, creating a seamless bridge from documentation to next steps — follow-ups, reminders, and shared team tasks, all grounded in the same problem-based structure.

At its core, Stream handles the administrative weight of medicine the same way it handles documentation itself:

**intelligently, transparently, and always under your command.**
